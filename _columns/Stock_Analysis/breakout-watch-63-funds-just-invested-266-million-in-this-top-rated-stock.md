After years of pressure from **Amazon.com** ([AMZN](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=AMZN)), [retail stocks](https://www.investors.com/news/retail-and-e-commerce-stocks-and-industry-news/) like **Kohl's** ([KSS](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=KSS)), **Target** ([TGT](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=TGT)) and TJ Maxx parent **TJX** ([TJX](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=TJX)) have been on the rise in recent months. And **VF Corp.** ([VFC](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=VFC)), which supplies retailers with its many apparel brands, is also benefiting and may be set to fashion a new breakout.




Along with discount retailers **Dollar General** ([DG](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=DG)) and **Ross Stores** ([ROST](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=ROST)), VF Corp. made the latest list of [new buys by the best mutual funds](https://www.investors.com/etfs-and-funds/mutual-funds/best-mutual-funds-buy-apple-adobe-ross/). Sixty-three of the top-performing funds from June to August invested approximately $266 million in the stock.


The owner of fashion brands such as Lee, Wrangler, The North Face, Dickies and JanSport sports a B Accumulation/Distribution Rating and 1.3 up/down volume ratio. VF Corp. has also posted three quarters of rising fund ownership, including a big jump from 1,369 to 1,494 funds from April to June.

Accelerating Growth
-------------------


VF Corp. has generated two consecutive quarters of rising top- and bottom-line growth. Earnings for its fiscal 2019 first quarter (ended June 30), jumped from 31% to 59%. Sales growth edged higher from 22% to 23%.


The North Carolina-based company has a strong 36.7% return on equity and an A [SMR Rating](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/dont-judge-a-companys-sales-in-a-vacuum-use-the-smr-rating-to-judge-growth/), which tracks sales growth, profit margins and ROE as a whole.


[Vans](https://www.vfc.com/news/press-releases/detail/1661/vans-brand-targets-5-billion-in-revenue-by-fiscal-year-2023), which was acquired by VF Corp. in 2004, recently said it plans to grow revenue by $2 billion to reach $5 billion in annual sales by fiscal 2023. For the quarter ended June 30, VF Corp. reported total revenue of $2.8 billion.


The company pays a dividend, currently yielding 2% on an annualized basis.


With a [95 Composite Rating](https://www.investors.com/ibd-data-stories/companies-now-outperforming-95-of-all-stocks/), VF Corp. holds the No. 2 spot ranking among apparel stocks. **Columbia Sportswear** ([COLM](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=COLM)) holds the No. 1 slot, while **Michael Kors** ([KORS](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=KORS)), **Canada Goose** ([GOOS](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=GOOS)) and **G-III Apparel** ([GIII](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=GIII)) round out the top five.


Trying To Fashion New Breakout
------------------------------


After topping analyst estimates in its latest quarterly report on July 20, the stock rose nearly 10% for that week. It's eased lower since then, and successfully tested support at its 10-week moving average.


VF Corp. is currently working on a flat base as part of a second-stage base-on-base formation. The buy point is 97.10. On Tuesday, the stock gapped up at the open and closed the day 1.3% higher at 92.35, leaving it 5% below the entry. Preliminary volume was below average.
