Wall Street's demand for artificial intelligence leaders like **Nvidia** ([NVDA](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=NVDA)) shows [no signs of waning](https://www.investors.com/etfs-and-funds/personal-finance/stock-market-enthusiasm-for-ai-stocks/). Now demand for AI is getting "cheesy" as **Domino's Pizza** ([DPZ](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=DPZ)) joins [Nvidia](https://www.investors.com/news/nvidia-stock-2023-buy-now/) on the this month's list of [new buys by the best mutual funds](https://www.investors.com/etfs-and-funds/mutual-funds/best-mutual-funds-nvidia-stock-netflix-cmg-aapl/). And as Nvidia rockets to yet another record high, DPZ stock looks to warm up a new breakout.




In October, Domino's Pizza announced a [collaboration on generative AI](https://ir.dominos.com/news-releases/news-release-details/dominosr-and-microsoft-cook-ai-driven-innovation-alliance) with **Microsoft** ([MSFT](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=MSFT)). The world's largest pizza company and the software and cloud computing giant aim to enhance the ordering process through personalization and simplification. With [Microsoft](https://www.investors.com/research/microsoft-stock-buy-now/) as its primary cloud provider, Domino's is leveraging the Microsoft Cloud and Azure OpenAI Service to deliver smarter pizza orders.


With AI, Domino's aims to help store managers save time on tasks such as inventory management, ingredient ordering and staff scheduling. Using generative AI technology, Domino's also plans to streamline pizza preparation and quality control with more predictive tools.

Domino's Pizza Delivers Growth
------------------------------


Founded with one store in Ypsilanti, Michigan in 1960, Domino's Pizza now has more than 20,700 stores in more than 90 markets. Its system is comprised of independent franchise owners who account for around 99% of Domino's stores.


Over the last four quarters, the company generated $18.5 billion in revenue. In its latest report on April 29, Domino's posted earnings of $3.58 a share. That marked a 22% year-over-year spike. In Q2, analysts forecast a 17% jump to $3.62 a share.


For the full year, Wall Street expects a 9% gain to $15.93 a share, followed by a 13% rise to $18.08 a share in 2025.


DPZ Stock Looks To Roll Out Fresh Breakout
------------------------------------------


From a year-ago low of 285.84 in June 2023, shares of Domino's Pizza nearly doubled to hit a high of 542.75 before pulling back to form its current base. As it now works on a second-stage flat base, DPZ stock stands 3% shy of the 542.75 [buy point](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/chart-reading-basics-how-a-buy-point-marks-a-time-of-opportunity/), established by the peek it in April.


Showing continued demand for the stock's impressive run, Domino's Pizza once again found support at its [10-week moving average](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/how-to-buy-stocks-why-the-10-week-moving-average-offers-new-entry-points/) at the end of May.


Further showcasing institutional demand, DPZ stock sports an A+ [Accumulation/Distribution Rating](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/how-to-buy-stocks-accumulation-distribution-rating-shows-professionals-moves/) and a 1.2 [up/down volume ratio](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/top-stocks-under-accumulation-use-the-up-down-volume-ratio-to-find-the-best-prospects/). Domino's Pizza earns a [92 Composite Rating](https://research.investors.com/stock-checkup/nyse-dominos-pizza-dpz.aspx). The rating means it is outpacing 92% of all stocks in terms of the most important [stock-picking factors](https://www.investors.com/research/stock-picks-best-stocks-to-buy-and-watch/).


Domino's closed lower Thursday and Friday but ended with a small gain for the week.
