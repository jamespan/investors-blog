Chip equipment maker **Advanced Energy Industries** ([AEIS](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=AEIS)) aims to power a new [breakout](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/what-is-stock-breakout/), boosted by accelerating earnings and sales growth. AEIS stock delivered a 138% spike in earnings last quarter, setting off a triangular-shaped base that's now targeting a new [buy point](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/the-best-stocks-have-crystal-clear-buy-points-heres-how-to-identify-them/) in a [cup with handle](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/the-basics-how-to-analyze-a-stocks-cup-with-handle/).




Advanced Energy ties industry group peer **Aehr Test Systems** ([AEHR](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=AEHR)) with a 98 [Composite Rating](https://www.investors.com/ibd-data-stories/stocks-to-watch-companies-with-top-stock-ratings/). Only **Axcelis Technologies** ([ACLS](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=ACLS)) scores higher in [Stock Checkup,](https://research.investors.com/stock-checkup/) holding the highest-possible 99 rating.


Other highly rated group leaders include **KLA** ([KLAC](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=KLAC)) and **Photronics** ([PLAB](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=PLAB)).


The [semiconductor](https://www.investors.com/news/technology/chip-stocks-and-semiconductor-industry-news-intel-qualcomm-qorvo/) equipment group ranks No. 49 among the 197 groups IBD tracks.


Advanced Energy In Demand Among Top Funds
-----------------------------------------


Denver-based Advanced Energy is a global leader in highly-engineered, precision power conversion, measurement and control solutions for mission-critical applications and processes. The company was founded in 1981, holds more than 700 patents and has over 11,000 employees.


The chip equipment business accounted for roughly half of revenue last quarter. Other major business segments include industrial electronics, flat panel displays, solar voltaics, medical applications, industrial coatings and more.


Over the last five quarters, AEIS stock has generated a sharp rebound in both its top and bottom-line performance.


Revenue has steadily increased since an 11% slowdown in Q3 2021, to 49% growth in Q3 2022. Earnings have fought back from a 46% contraction, to a 138% spike, over the same period,


Analysts now forecast 35% earnings growth for the current year, slowing down an estimated 20% in 2023.


Indicating institutional demand for Advanced Energy, 38 funds with an "A+" rating from IBD own shares in AEIS stock, which has a 1.3 up/down volume ratio.


AEIS Stock Engineers New Buy Point
----------------------------------


Advanced Energy entered a downward slide in January 2021 and bottomed out this summer. AEIS stock lifted off lows and now trades around 4% below a 94.93 [buy point](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/chart-reading-basics-how-a-buy-point-marks-a-time-of-opportunity/) in a [cup with handle](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/the-basics-spot-traits-of-proper-handles-on-cup-patterns/).


Confirming the stock's rebounding technical strength, its [50-day moving average](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/what-is-the-50-day-moving-average-when-to-buy-or-sell-growth-stocks/) has edged back above its 200-day line. Also note how AEIS stock found support at its [21-day line](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/what-is-the-21-day-exponential-moving-average/) while forming the handle.


See if Advanced Energy can ride these positive developments and deliver a heavy-volume breakout.
