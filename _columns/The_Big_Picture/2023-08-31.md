The stock market finished mixed Thursday, as Wall Street digested an inflation report. Next up on Friday, investors will shift their attention to the Labor Department's August jobs report.




As the new stock market uptrend continues, watch tech titan **Nvidia** ([NVDA](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=NVDA)), new [IBD Leaderboard](https://www.investors.com/product/leaderboard/?artProdLink=Leaderboard) stock **Quanta Services** ([PWR](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=PWR)), **Shopify** ([SHOP](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SHOP)), **Synopsys** ([SNPS](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SNPS)) and Dow Jones payments giant **Visa** ([V](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=V)).


Inflation Data, Jobs Report
---------------------------


The [personal consumption expenditures](https://www.investors.com/news/economy/federal-reserve-key-inflation-rate-cooling-trend-intact-sp-500-rises/), or PCE, price index rose 0.2% in July. Still, the annual inflation rate rose to 3.3% from 3% in June. The increase in inflation largely reflected an especially low reading in July 2022 falling out of the 12-month calculation.


Also, the Labor Department's initial jobless claims fell to 228,000 vs. 238,000 in the previous week. And the monthly job cuts report from Challenger, Gray & Christmas showed employers announced 75,151 layoffs in August. That was a 217% increase over June and a 267% jump vs. year-ago levels.


U.S. Treasury yields ticked lower, with the 10-year yield at 4.09% late Thursday, the lowest since Aug. 10.


![market pulse](https://www.investors.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/MP_1x_090423-221x300.jpg)The August jobs report is out Friday morning. Wall Street expects nonfarm payrolls to grow by 170,000 in July, down from June's 187,000 increase. The unemployment rate is expected to remain unchanged at 3.5%.


On the earnings front Thursday, **CrowdStrike** ([CRWD](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=CRWD)), **Dollar General** ([DG](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=DG)), **Okta** ([OKTA](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=OKTA)) and **Veeva Systems** ([VEEV](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=VEEV)) were key earnings movers.


[CrowdStrike shares](https://www.investors.com/news/technology/crwd-stock-okta-stock-crowdstrike-earnings-okta-earnings-q22023/) advanced 9.3% Thursday, while OKTA stock jumped more than 13%. DG stock plunged 12%, and Veeva stock soared more than 8%, just shy of a flat base's 211.95 buy point.


Stock Market Today
------------------


On Thursday, the Dow Jones Industrial Average dropped 0.5%, while the S&P 500 moved down almost 0.2% The tech-heavy Nasdaq composite rose just 0.1%. Volume rose on the Nasdaq, which gave the composite a [stalling day](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/stalling-signals-an-early-warning-for-stock-market-heres-how-to-find-this-insidious-signal/), a form of distribution.


The small-cap Russell 2000 lost 0.2%.


In the Dow 30, Salesforce was the session's big winner. [CRM stock](https://www.investors.com/news/technology/crm-stock-salesforce-stock-salesforce-earnings-q32023/) advanced 3% on earnings to retake its 50-day line. A cup base is forming with a 238.22 buy point.


Top growth stocks finished higher, as the Innovator IBD 50 ETF ([FFTY](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=FFTY)) rose nearly 0.6%, extending a win streak to five sessions Thursday. In the [IBD 50](https://research.investors.com/stock-lists/ibd-50/), **Arista Networks** ([ANET](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=ANET)) rallied 4.3%, hitting a new high and moving further past a 178.36 buy point.


With this week's bullish action, IBD's market outlook was upgraded to a confirmed uptrend. IBD's recommended exposure range was upped to 20%-40%. Investors should be putting their watch lists to work. But raise exposure methodically, especially with the risk that economic data could rattle investors.
