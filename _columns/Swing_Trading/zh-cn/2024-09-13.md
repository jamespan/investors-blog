The future is very hard to predict, which is why we focus on identifying the current trend and reacting accordingly. The recent [expectation breakers](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/growth-stocks-expectation-breakers-what-to-do-when-stock-market-throws-you-curveball/) on the S&P 500 suggested potential trend changes rather than normal deviations. Those are important and they can be positive or negative.  
未来很难预测，这就是为什么我们专注于识别当前趋势并做出相应的反应。标准普尔 500 指数最近[打破预期表明](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/growth-stocks-expectation-breakers-what-to-do-when-stock-market-throws-you-curveball/)潜在的趋势变化，而不是正常偏差。这些都很重要，它们可以是积极的，也可以是消极的。




Negative Expectation Breakers Are Not To Be Ignored消极期望打破者不容忽视
--------------------------------------------------------------


The challenge with following trends is they don't happen in straight lines. You might be able to identify the trend with a single line but you expect some variation from the average. Sometimes a variation is more pronounced than others. That can make a normal pullback in the S&P 500 raise a question: Is this a normal deviation from the mean or a change in trend?  
追随趋势的挑战在于它们不会直线发生。您可能能够用一条线来识别趋势，但您预计与平均值会有一些变化。有时，一种变化比其他变化更明显。这可能会使标准普尔 500 指数的正常回调引发一个问题：这是对平均值的正常偏差还是趋势的变化？


We favor using the SPDR S&P 500 ([SPY](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SPY)) exchange traded fund to represent the action of the S&P 500. [This column previously addressed an expectation breaker in August.](https://www.investors.com/research/swing-trading/expectation-breaker-sp-500-spy/)  
我们赞成使用 SPDR S&P 500 （[SPY](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SPY)） 交易所交易基金来代表 S&P 500 的行动。[本专栏之前在 8 月份讨论了一个打破预期的问题。](https://www.investors.com/research/swing-trading/expectation-breaker-sp-500-spy/)


To recap, a minor 5% pullback in the S&P 500 crossed below the 50-day moving average line **(1)**. After a weak bounce, the index triggered a [follow-through day](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/follow-through-signals-market-uptrend/) a few days later signaling a change in the trend **(2)**. The expectation is that if the trend truly changed, you will quickly get momentum in the direction of the new trend.  
回顾一下，标准普尔 500 指数小幅回调 5% 跌破 50 天移动平均线 **（1）。**在疲软反弹之后，该指数在几天后触发了[后续日](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/follow-through-signals-market-uptrend/)，表明趋势发生变化 **（2）。**预期是，如果趋势真的发生了变化，您将很快朝着新趋势的方向获得动力。

That's not what happened. The next day was a big outside day closing near the lows **(3)**. It was a negative expectation breaker. We went from 91% invested down to 21% by the close. If we hadn't done aggressive selling then we would have certainly taken a larger drawdown as the downtrend took another sharp leg down **(4)**.  
事实并非如此。第二天是一个大的外围日收于低点**附近 （3）。**这是一个消极的期望打破者。我们的投资比例从 91% 下降到收盘时的 21%。如果我们没有进行激进的抛售，那么我们肯定会采取更大的回撤，因为下降趋势再次大幅下跌 **（4）。**


Change As New Data Comes In随着新数据的传入而变化
--------------------------------------


Since we were barely invested at the Aug. 5 bottom, we were unaffected by the panic selling of the morning. The question then becomes, when to get back in? On Aug. 13, we got another follow-through day in the S&P 500 **(5)**. We steadily increased our exposure, including a position in the ProShares Ultra S&P 500 ETF ([SSO](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SSO)) on [SwingTrader](http://shop.investors.com/offer/splashresponsive.aspx?id=SwingTrader&src=A011LPH). The ETF adds leverage by doubling the move of the S&P 500.  
由于我们几乎没有投资于 8 月 5 日的底部，因此我们没有受到上午恐慌性抛售的影响。那么问题就变成了，什么时候重新进来？8 月 13 日，标准普尔 500 指数 **（5）** 再次出现后续交易日。我们稳步增加我们的敞口，包括在 [SwingTrader](http://shop.investors.com/offer/splashresponsive.aspx?id=SwingTrader&src=A011LPH) 上持有 ProShares Ultra S&P 500 ETF （[SSO）](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SSO) 的头寸。该 ETF 通过将标准普尔 500 指数的波动翻倍来增加杠杆。


As the index pushed ahead, we saw steady gains. It culminated in another positive signal when the S&P 500 triggered a power trend rule from our Market School program **(6)**. That signals a new trend is well established and our focus shifts to holding winners and getting more money into them. Instead we got another negative expectation breaker **(7)**. We cut our exposure in half immediately and continued lightening through the end of the week as the S&P 500 fell further **(8)**. Interestingly, it nearly matched the level reached on the first breach of the 50-day line **(1)**.  
随着指数向前推进，我们看到了稳步上涨。当标准普尔 500 指数触发我们的市场学校计划 **（6）** 的实力趋势规则时，它最终产生了另一个积极信号。这表明新趋势已经确立，我们的重点转移到持有赢家并从中获得更多资金。相反，我们得到了另一个负期望打破者 **（7）。**我们立即将风险敞口减半，并在标准普尔 500 指数进一步下跌的情况下继续减持至周末 **（8）。**有趣的是，它几乎与首次突破 50 日线 **（1）** 时达到的水平相匹配。


Why This S&P 500 Expectation Breaker Was Different为什么这个标准普尔 500 指数预期打破者与众不同
---------------------------------------------------------------------------


That line around 540 became a battleground. If the bears were truly in control, the expectation is that level wouldn't hold. Instead we got a positive expectation breaker **(9)**.  
540 左右的那条线变成了战场。如果空头真的控制了局面，预计该水平不会持续。相反，我们得到了一个正预期破坏者 **（9）。**


When the battle lines were tested, not only did they hold but the bulls advanced the S&P 500 past the Sept. 6 high and approached the Aug. 1 high **(3)**. So far, that advance is continuing rather than being reversed.  
当战线受到考验时，它们不仅守住了，而且多头将标准普尔 500 指数推高了 9 月 6 日的高点，并接近 8 月 1 日的高点 **（3）。**到目前为止，这种进步正在继续，而不是被逆转。


Now the battle lines have been redrawn in favor of the bulls. We increased back to full exposure on SwingTrader by repurchasing our leveraged S&P 500 ETF. It's not always easy keeping up with market gyrations but recognizing early signals with expectation breakers can start your momentum before a trend is too obvious and too far to profit.  
现在，战线已经重新绘制，有利于多头。我们通过回购杠杆 S&P 500 ETF 增加了 SwingTrader 的全部敞口。跟上市场波动并不总是那么容易，但识别具有预期破坏者的早期信号可以在趋势太明显和太远而无法获利之前开始您的势头。
