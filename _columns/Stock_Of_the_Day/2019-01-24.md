**Domino's Pizza** ([DPZ](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=DPZ)) is the [IBD Stock of the Day](https://www.investors.com/research/ibd-stock-of-the-day/), flashing a bullish technical sign Thursday as it moves to a buy point. An improving restaurant sector has hoisted Domino's stock as well as larger peers **McDonald's** ([MCD](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=MCD)), **Yum Brands** ([YUM](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=YUM)) and **Starbucks** ([SBUX](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SBUX)) near buy points.




Domino's Pizza is the second largest pizza restaurant chain in the world, selling more than 1.5 million pizzas each day. What began as one store in 1960 is now 15,300 store locations in more than 85 global markets.


A longtime industry leader, Domino's earnings and revenue derive from royalties paid by its franchisees. It also runs a number of its own stores.


Domino's Stock Technical Analysis
---------------------------------


Domino's stock rose 1.4% to 276.98 on the [stock market today](https://www.investors.com/market-trend/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-market-trends-best-stocks-buy-watch/), snapping a downward-sloping trend line. Shares are continuing a five-day surge, reclaiming their 50-day and 200-day moving averages. Domino's stock is working on the right side of a first-stage consolidation with a potential 305.44 buy point, according to [MarketSmith analysis](https://marketsmith.investors.com/).



The [relative strength line](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/relative-strength-line-identifies-strong-stocks/), which tracks a stock's performance vs. the S&P 500 index, is at consolidation highs and right at the June 2018 peak. That RS peak ended a seven-year run of Domino's stock outperformance.

IBD Stock Of The Day Fundamentals
---------------------------------


Domino's stock boasts superior fundamentals, which are reflected in its [IBD Composite Rating](https://education.investors.com/financial-dictionary/general/composite-rating-cma--smartselect-rgr-) of 95 out of a best-possible 99. It earns an [EPS Rating](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/eps-rating-is-key-to-picking-great-stocks/) of 97.


Domino's earnings rose at a 34% annual clip over the past three years. Annual sales increased at a 15% pace over the same period. Domino's earnings have been fueled by the company's early embrace of mobile ordering


Annual EPS growth above 25% for the past three years is a key [CAN SLIM](https://www.investors.com/ibd-university/can-slim/) requirement. Looking forward, analysts see EPS growth slowing to 13% in 2019 from an estimated 47% in 2018.


Domino's Stock Peers Faring Well
--------------------------------


Meanwhile, the retail-restaurant group currently ranks No. 39 out of 197 industry groups tracked by IBD. That's up from No. 66 four weeks ago. Several fast-food or quick-serve chains are doing well in addition to Domino's Pizza. McDonald's stock, Starbucks stock, **Wingstop** ([WING](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=WING)) and Yum Brands — which owns Taco Bell, KFC and Pizza Hut — are near buy points. Starbucks stock, which is on [IBD Leaderboard](https://leaderboard.investors.com//#/leaders/leadersnearabuypoint), reported earnings late Thursday.


**Chipotle Mexican Grill** ([CMG](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=CMG)) is slightly extended from a [double-bottom base](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/what-is-double-bottom-base-biotech-stocks/).
