Helping businesses make better and faster decisions derived from their data is the mission of **New Relic** ([NEWR](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=NEWR)), the [IBD Stock Of The Day](https://www.investors.com/research/ibd-stock-of-the-day/).







The company provides a cloud-based suite of software products that enable organizations to collect, store, and analyze massive amounts of software data in real time. It's a category called software analytics.


New Relic stock is [forming a flat base](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/when-to-buy-the-basics-of-a-flat-base-a-super-growth-stock-pattern/) and is currently below the potential buy point of 114.88, which would be a new record high for the software-as-a-service provider. Shares of New Relic closed Friday at 106.93, down 0.7% on the [stock market today](https://www.investors.com/market-trend/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-market-trends-best-stocks-buy-watch/). New Relic stock is up 85% this year. The company is on the exclusive [IBD Leaderboard](https://leaderboard.investors.com/#/leaders/leadersnearabuypoint) list of top-performing companies.


In December 2014 New Relic held its [initial public offering](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1448056/000119312514406260/d709327ds1.htm), pricing shares at 23. Shares popped 48% on the first day of trading.


New Relic helps its customers gain increased visibility into their enterprise software to help make better and faster data-driven decisions. Its cloud platform makes every aspect of modern software and infrastructure observable, so companies can find and fix problems faster and speed up business projects.


New Relic Stock Remains Strong
------------------------------


New Relic stock is in the third stage of a [flat base](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/how-to-trade-stocks-base-stock-charts/), after a huge run that began April 2017. While another big gain would be difficult to achieve, the stock remains strong in terms of fundamentals.


Needham analyst Jack Andrews, in a note to clients Wednesday said he continues to view New Relic as a "next-generation category leader." He has a buy rating on New Relic and price target of 129. The company is enjoying broad customer interest, steady regional expansion and expanding its new products.


For its [fiscal first quarter](https://ir.newrelic.com/press-releases/Press-Release-Details/2018/New-Relic-Announces-First-Quarter-Fiscal-Year-2019-Results/) ended June 30, New Relic reported revenue of $108.2 million, up 35% from the year-ago period. It's posted 17 quarters in a row of double-digit revenue growth. It reported adjusted earnings of 15 cents, vs. a loss of 9 cents the same quarter a year ago. The company reported having cash and equivalents of $721 million.


For the current quarter, New Relic expects revenue in the range of $110.5 million to $112.5 million, up about 32%. It expects adjusted earnings in the range of 11 cents to 12 cents. That compares with a 6-cent per share loss in the year-ago period. The company says it has more than 17,000 customers.
