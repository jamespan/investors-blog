**Etsy** ([ETSY](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=ETSY)) is the [IBD Stock of the Day](https://www.investors.com/research/ibd-stock-of-the-day/), an e-commerce company that sells handmade goods, vintage items and other arts and crafts. Etsy stock soared to a new high Tuesday.




Shares soared more than 7% at one point but settled back to close at 55.44, up 1.3% on the [stock market today](https://www.investors.com/market-trend/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-market-trends-best-stocks-buy-watch/). The surge occurred without any apparent headline news that might have fueled the stock's ascent.


Etsy broke out of a [cup-with-handle base](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/the-basics-how-to-analyze-a-stocks-cup-with-handle/) past its buy point of 56.52, with a buy range up to 59.35. The company was added to the exclusive [IBD Leaderboard](https://leaderboard.investors.com/#/leaders/leadersnearabuypoint) list of top-performing stocks on Tuesday.


The company plans to report fourth-quarter earnings on Feb. 25, after the market close. Wall Street expects Etsy to report adjusted earnings of 21 cents a share. That would be a 40% rise from the year-ago period.

Etsy Stock Soars On Earnings
----------------------------


Etsy stock soared 24% when it reported [third-quarter earnings](https://www.investors.com/news/technology/etsy-stock-earnings/) on Nov. 6 that beat estimates. Etsy also raised revenue guidance for the year.



Gross merchandise sales topped $922.5 million in the third quarter, up 20% from the year-ago period. Etsy also announced the approval of a plan to repurchase up to $200 million of its common stock.

Top Rated Among Peers
---------------------


The company also is the top-rated stock among its peers in IBD's Retail/Internet industry group, followed by **Alibaba** ([BABA](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=BABA)), **Amazon** ([AMZN](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=AMZN)) and **eBay** ([EBAY](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=EBAY)).


Etsy held its initial public offering in April 2015, pricing shares at 16. The stock popped 72% on its first day of trading to 35.74.


From that point Etsy stock, in its first year of trading, had plunged to its all-time low of 6.04. Since then, string of better-than-expected earnings reports has lifted Etsy stock, which is up 230% in the past 12 months.
