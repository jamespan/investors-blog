**Crocs** ([CROX](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=CROX)) is the [**IBD Stock Of The Day**](https://www.investors.com/category/research/ibd-stock-of-the-day/) as the foam clog maker continues to churn out new products in collaboration with pop-culture partners. CROX stock hit a new high and is in buy range. 




The Broomfield, Colo.-based company has a cultlike following among Gen Z social media followers. Crocs has scored partnerships with some of the most popular celebrities, including pop star Justin Bieber and Latin sensation Bad Bunny. It also has deals with rapper Post Malone and county singer Luke Combs.


Most recently, Crocs teamed up with luxury fashion brand Balenciaga. The fashion house featured Crocs stilettos, which reportedly cost $1,000 a pair, at its spring 2022 collection. The high-heeled stilettos come in black and bright green.


Crocs also inked a deal with Russian band Little Big on an exclusive punk-rock-inspired collaboration featuring the brand's Classic Clog with metal spikes, chains and Jibbitz charms inspired by the band's unique style. The clogs will be available on June 22 in pink and black versions. They will retail for around $90 for the pink version and $98 for the black ones.


Meanwhile, it's partnership with Grammy-winning DJ and producer Diplo yielded two versions of limited-edition Diplo X Crocs. One version has a swirly pink, blue and yellow design with 3D mushroom charms that light up. It retails for $70. The other design is a two-strap, slip-on sandal with eight detachable glow-in-the-dark charms priced at $50. Both went on sale June 8.


Crox Stock
----------


Shares [gapped up](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/why-buying-great-stocks-on-a-gap-up-is-sound-investing-strategy/) 3.6% to a new closing high of 112.58 on the [stock market today](https://www.investors.com/category/market-trend/stock-market-today/). CROX stock cleared a [buy point](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/chart-reading-basics-how-a-buy-point-marks-a-time-of-opportunity/) of 110.01 from a [flat base](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/flat-base-stock-dollar-tree-breakout/) on a weekly chart, according to [MarketSmith chart analysis](https://www.investors.com/product/marketsmith/?artProdLink=MarketSmith). The [chase zone](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/buy-zone-nvidia-stock/) extends to 115.51.


Croc's [relative strength line](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/a-stock-breakout-specialty-tool-the-relative-strength-line/) is trending upward and hit a new high on Monday. Its [RS Rating](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/relative-strength-rating-stock-chart-analysis-helps-pick-outstanding-growth-stocks/) is a 96 out of a possible 99. The company's [EPS Rating](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/eps-rating-is-key-to-picking-great-stocks/) is a top-notch 99 as it's posted four straight quarters of earnings growth.


The stock has had a couple of volume spikes since reporting earnings on April 27. Shares gapped up 15% on that day and climbed all the way to a high of 109.91 on May 7.


Rival **Skechers** ([SKX](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SKX)) was down 2% at 48.96. SKX stock is closing in on a buy point of 53.24 from a flat base on a weekly chart.


Crocs Earnings
--------------


In April, Crocs crushed first-quarter estimates, with soaring profits and strong sales across all segments. EPS shot up 577% to $1.49, beating views for 88 cents. Revenue surged 64% to $460.1 million, above estimates for $415.4 million. Digital sales surged 75.3%.


"Sales of clogs were exceptional this quarter, increasing 87% year over year, representing 76% of total footwear revenues vs. 65% last year," said [Crocs CEO Andrew Rees](https://www.investors.com/news/management/leaders-and-success/ceo-andrew-rees-led-the-powerful-resurgence-of-crocs/) on an earnings call. "We continue to experience success with seasonal offerings and trend-right drops, such as out-of-this-world and marble prints."


Management also gave a rosy outlook for Q2 and the second half of the year. Crocs sees second-quarter sales growing 60% to 70% year over year, well above the 40% growth rate analysts forecast. It also sees 2021 sales growing up to 50%, nearly double views for 24.6%.


Nearly all of the analysts polled by FactSet boosted their price targets and earnings estimates for CROX stock following earnings results.


Pivotal Research Group analyst Mitch Kummetz, who has a buy rating on the stock, said in a note to clients on April 27 that the outlook for the second half of 2021 reflects "normalizing seasonality, continued back-to-school momentum, good visibility on wholesale orders and price increases."


Kummetz raised his price target for CROX stock to 125 from 100 and his EPS estimate to $1.53 from $1.17.


On average, analysts sees Crocs EPS of $1.52 for the second quarter, a 50% increase from the year-ago period. They expect sales to jump 68% to $558 million. Internet sales are seen climbing 67% to $211 million.
