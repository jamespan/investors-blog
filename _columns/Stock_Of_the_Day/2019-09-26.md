**First Solar** ([FSLR](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=FSLR)) is the [IBD Stock Of The Day](https://www.investors.com/category/research/ibd-stock-of-the-day/), a solar panel maker that is riding a wave of enthusiasm for solar industry stocks. First Solar stock is in the eighth week of a [cup base](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/investing-202-why-some-great-cup-bases-dont-form-a-handle/), with a buy point of 69.33.




Founded in 1999 and headquartered in Tempe, Ariz., First Solar specializes in high-efficiency thin-film solar panels used mainly in utility-scale and some commercial installations. It also operates a downstream project development business.


First Solar stock recently broke above its [50-day moving average](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/when-to-sell-stocks-big-break-below-50-day-line-can-mark-end-of-a-huge-run/). But a tough day for the sector left it down 6.8% to close at 62.54 on the [stock market today](https://www.investors.com/market-trend/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-market-trends-best-stocks-buy-watch/).


The IBD [Stock Checkup Tool](https://research.investors.com/stock-checkup/) shows that First Solar has a healthy [IBD Composite Rating](https://education.investors.com/financial-dictionary/general/composite-rating-cma--smartselect-rgr-) of 81 out of 99. Its [Relative Strength Rating](https://education.investors.com/financial-dictionary/general/relative-price-strength--op-rs-cp--rating-or-relative-strength) is 93, which reflects strong [stock market performance](https://www.investors.com/market-trend/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-market-trends-best-stocks-buy-watch/).

First Solar shares have advanced 55% this year. Forecasts call for company earnings to jump 80% this year and 48% in 2020.


The 17-stock IBD Energy-Solar industry group has a year-to-date gain of about 81%, after losing 25% of its value in 2018 as tariffs and pricing issues rattled the market. The solar energy group ranks No. 2 among [IBD's 197 industries](https://www.investors.com/data-tables/industry-group-rankings-sep-17-2019/), based on six-month relative price performance.


Solar Industry Leaders
----------------------


The leading stock in the IBD Energy-Solar industry group is [Leaderboard](https://leaderboard.investors.com) name **SolarEdge Technologies** ([SEDG](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SEDG)), followed by **Enphase Energy** ([ENPH](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=ENPH)), then **Daqo New Energy** ([DQ](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=DQ)) and **JinkoSolar** ([JKS](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=JKS)). First Solar stock is fifth.


Other solar industry names include **SunPower** ([SPWR](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=SPWR)), **Sunrun** ([RUN](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=RUN)) and **Vivint Solar** ([VSLR](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=VSLR)). SunPower was an [IBD Stock Of The Day](https://www.investors.com/research/ibd-stock-of-the-day/sunpower-stock-ibd-stock-of-day-shines-bright-in-hot-market/) last week.


First Solar's second-quarter results on Aug. 1 showed an 89% jump in revenue from the year-ago period to $585 million. It had an adjusted loss of 18 cents per share vs. a loss of 46 cents. Bookings were stronger than expected.


First Solar missed top- and bottom-line expectations for the second quarter but maintained revenue and earnings guidance for 2019.


The company expects 2019 revenue in the range of $3.5 billion to $3.7 billion. Consensus of analysts tracking First Solar stock is $3.57 billion. The midpoint of $3.6 billion represents a 63% jump from the prior year.


It expects adjusted earnings in the range of $2.25 to $2.75 a share vs. analyst predictions for $2.42.


"We continued to make significant progress in our Series 6 transition during (the second quarter), with improvements across all manufacturing metrics," Chief Executive Mark Widmar said in written remarks with the earnings report. "We had record module production and shipments, and with our recent bookings success we are now essentially sold out through 2020, with significant bookings visibility into 2021."


Outperform Rating On First Solar Stock
--------------------------------------


Baird analyst Ben Kallo has a rating of outperform and a price target of 80 on First Solar stock.


"First Solar is our favorite name in the solar space and a top idea overall," Kallo wrote in a recent note to clients.


"We like First Solar due to its strong pipeline, continued cost and efficiency improvements, and strong balance sheet which will fund the ramp of Series 6 and provide growth optionality moving forward," he wrote.


Series 6 is a new line of solar panels with improved performance and reputed higher reliability than older First Solar modules.
