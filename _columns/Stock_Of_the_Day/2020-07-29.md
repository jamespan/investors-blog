**Monolithic Power Systems** ([MPWR](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=MPWR)) is the [IBD Stock Of The Day](https://www.investors.com/category/research/ibd-stock-of-the-day/) as the chipmaker outpaces its peers thanks to growth from cloud data centers and 5G wireless infrastructure. MPWR stock surged to a record high Wednesday after the company delivered a beat-and-raise quarterly earnings report.




Late Tuesday, the Kirkland, Wash.-based company reported [second-quarter results](https://www.investors.com/news/technology/monolithic-earnings-q2-2020-mpwr-stock/) that topped expectations. Monolithic earned an adjusted $1.08 a share, up 17% year over year, on sales of $186.2 million, up 23%. Analysts expected Monolithic earnings of $1 a share on sales of $169.8 million.


It was the company's third-straight quarter of accelerating sales and earnings growth.


For the current quarter, Monolithic expects sales to rise 21% year over year to $205 million. That's based on the midpoint of its outlook. Wall Street had predicted a sales increase of 8% to $182.4 million in the third quarter.

MPWR Stock Clears Ascending Base
--------------------------------


On the [stock market today](https://www.investors.com/market-trend/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-market-trends-best-stocks-buy-watch/), MPWR stock jumped 6.6% to close at 263.88. Earlier in the session, it broke out of a rare [ascending base](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/rare-ascending-base-can-lead-to-powerful-gains/) with a [buy point](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/chart-reading-basics-how-a-buy-point-marks-a-time-of-opportunity/) of 255.79, according to [IBD MarketSmith charts](https://www.investors.com/product/marketsmith/?artProdLink=MarketSmith).


In intraday trading, MPWR stock notched an all-time high of 264.29.


In a bullish sign, the [relative strength line](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/a-stock-breakout-specialty-tool-the-relative-strength-line/) for MPWR stock is at a record high as shares outperform the benchmark S&P 500 index.


Monolithic Power Systems is tied for first place in IBD's Electronics-Semiconductor Fabless industry group. It shares a best-possible [IBD Composite Rating](https://www.investors.com/how-to-invest/investors-corner/how-to-research-growth-stocks/) of 99 with **AMD** ([AMD](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=AMD)) and **Nvidia** ([NVDA](https://research.investors.com/quote.aspx?symbol=NVDA)), according to the [IBD Stock Checkup](https://research.investors.com/stock-checkup/nasdaq-monolithic-power-systems-mpwr.aspx) tool. The Composite Rating scores a stock's key growth metrics against all other stocks regardless of industry group.


Monolithic Power Systems Gets Price-Target Hikes
------------------------------------------------


A flurry of Wall Street analysts raised their price targets on MPWR stock after the company's June-quarter earnings report.


Deutsche Bank analyst Ross Seymore reiterated his buy rating on MPWR stock. He also upped his price target to 270 from 255.


He sees a "wide array of company-specific growth drivers" in the second half of 2020 and into 2021. They include automotive chip wins, video game console production ramp, data center content growth and 5G infrastructure buildout, Seymore said.


Monolithic sells energy-efficient semiconductor power systems used in industrial applications, telecom infrastructure, cloud computing, automotive and consumer applications.
